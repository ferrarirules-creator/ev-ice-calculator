🚗 EV vs ICE Ownership Cost Calculator
Compare lifetime Ownership costs and savings between Electric and ICE vehicles

📖 How to Use This Calculator (Click to expand)

▼

1 Enter Driving Parameters

City/Highway Split: Enter percentage (e.g., 80% city, 20% highway).
Monthly Running: Your average monthly kilometers.
Ownership Duration: How long you plan to keep the vehicle (in years).

2 Select Your State/UT

Choose your state from the dropdown. This automatically updates fuel prices, electricity rates, and RTO charges specific to your location. If you don't find a value correct, you can edit based on your convenience

3 Fill Vehicle Details

EV Side: Enter ex-showroom price, discounts, efficiency (km/kWh).
ICE Side: Select fuel type (Petrol/Diesel), enter ex-showroom price, discounts, and mileage (km/l).
All fields update calculations in real-time!

4 Optional: Add Loan Details

If buying on loan, enter interest rate (%) and tenure (months). Leave empty if paying cash. The calculator shows savings with and without loan costs.

5 View Results & Download

Scroll down to see comprehensive comparison: running costs, savings, investment returns, and resale value. Click "📸 Download" to save results as an image

💡 Quick Tips

  • RTO auto-calculates based on state and vehicle price
  • Green values mean savings with EV; red means ICE is cheaper
  • Investment ROI shows returns if you invest monthly fuel savings
  • Reset button (top) clears all fields to start over
  • Mobile friendly - swipe table left/right to see all columns

📋 Assumptions & Notes

🔧 Maintenance Costs:

Maintenance costs have not been compared as they vary OEM to OEM. EVs in general have lower maintenance costs than ICE cars.

🔋 Battery Warranty & Longevity:

EV batteries are under warranty for Lifetime (15 years) and unlimited kms or they get 8 years or 1,60,000 kms. Hence there is no need to consider battery replacement costs. EV batteries have high cycle life so they are good for 3,00,000 kms easily.

⛽ Fuel & Electricity Prices:

Fuel and Electricity prices have been assumed to be constant as the same basic price inflation will apply to both.
