EV vs ICE: Complete Guide to Understanding Total Cost of Ownership

By Ankush Published: November 1, 2025 Updated: November 2, 2025

Whenever you talk to someone about an EV, the first thing they mention is the higher upfront cost. But is that the complete picture? Let's dive deep into understanding the total cost of ownership.

Why Upfront Cost Doesn't Tell the Whole Story

When comparing electric vehicles (EVs) with internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles, focusing solely on the ex-showroom price is misleading. The Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) includes:

  • Purchase price (ex-showroom + RTO + insurance + TCS)
  • Loan interest (if financing)
  • Fuel/electricity costs over ownership period
  • Maintenance and servicing
  • Depreciation

Breaking Down the Costs

1. Registration and Taxes (RTO)

State-wise RTO charges vary significantly:

  • Electric Vehicles: 0% road tax in some states and lower than ICE Road tax in other states
  • Petrol/Diesel: 8-18% road tax
Example: A ₹15 lakh EV in Delhi pays ~₹16,000 RTO, while a ₹12 lakh petrol car pays ~₹1.2 lakh. The EV RTO is often lower percentage-wise.

2. Fuel Costs: The Game Changer

This is where EVs shine:

  • Petrol: ₹100-110/liter (varies by state)
  • Diesel: ₹90-95/liter
  • Electricity: ₹6-10/unit (home charging)

Monthly Running Example (1000 km):

  • Petrol car (15 km/l): ~₹7,000/month
  • EV (6 km/kWh): ~₹1,500/month
  • Savings: ₹5,500/month or ₹66,000/year

3. The Breakeven Point

Most EVs reach breakeven within 2-4 years depending on:

  • Monthly running distance (higher = faster breakeven)
  • Ownership duration
  • State fuel prices and RTO rates
  • Loan terms and interest rates

Real-World Example: Tata Nexon EV vs Nexon Petrol

Scenario:

  • Nexon EV Empowered+: ₹14.5 lakh (ex-showroom)
  • Nexon Petrol Fearless Plus DCA: ₹12.8 lakh
  • Monthly running: 1000 km
  • Ownership: 5 years
  • State: Maharashtra

Results:

  • EV has higher upfront cost by ₹1.7 lakh
  • EV saves ₹66,000/year in fuel (₹3.3 lakh over 5 years)
  • Lower maintenance saves additional ₹50,000
  • Total savings over 5 years: ₹1.8+ lakh
  • Breakeven: Month 31 (2.5 years)

Key Factors That Affect TCO

1. Monthly Running Distance

The more you drive, the faster EVs pay for themselves.

2. Ownership Duration

EVs favor longer ownership. 5+ years shows maximum benefit due to cumulative fuel savings.

3. Charging Infrastructure

Home charging is crucial. Public charging costs can be 2-3x higher but still it ends up being cheaper than an ICE car

4. State-Specific Policies

RTO rates, subsidies, and fuel prices vary by state. Always check local costs.

Common Myths Debunked

Myth: "EVs are too expensive"

Reality: Several EVs are now price-competitive with ICE, and all EVs are cheaper over 3-5 years when TCO is considered.

Myth: "Battery replacement will cost a fortune"

Reality: Modern EV batteries have 8-10 year warranties. Most retain 80%+ capacity after 5 years with minimal degradation.

Conclusion: Make an Informed Decision

The question isn't whether EVs are expensive—it's whether they make sense for your specific use case. Factors to consider:

  • ✅ Drive 700+ km/month? EV is likely cheaper
  • ✅ Have home charging access? EV economics improve significantly
  • ✅ Plan to keep car 5+ years? Maximum EV benefit
  • ✅ High fuel prices in your state? EVs save more
